Beef Birria Tacos

Description

I love the vibrant flavors of Mexican cuisine. One of my favorites has to be Birria tacos. They're flavorful and juicy and just outright delicious!

If you're looking for something that gives you a nice crispy bite into its succulent insides, birria tacos are the way to go. Are you ready to give them a try?

Ingredients

Meat

  • 4 lbs. beef chuck roast
  • 1 tsp onion powder
  • salt and pepper to taste

Sauce

  • 6 dried guajillo chile peppers, seeded
  • 4 dried chile de arbol peppers, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 dried ancho chiles, stemmed and seeded
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 medium Roma tomatoes
  • 2 tbsp white vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, chopped
  • 2 tsp black pepper
  • 4 whole cloves
  • pinch of ground cinnamon
  • pinch of ground cumin
  • pinch of ground thyme
  • pinch of dried marjoram
  • pinch of dried oregano
  • pinch of salt

Tacos

  • 18 corn tortillas
  • 1 large white onion, finely chopped
  • 1 bunch of cilantro, chopped
  • 1 cup shredded queso asadero (white Mexican cheese)

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 325 degrees (ºF). Fill a pot with water and bring to a boil.

  2. Add the chile peppers to the boiling water and boil for about 5 minutes. Remove from heat and allow peppers to soak until cool. Drain and reserve 1/4 cup of the cooking water.

  3. Rinse the beef and pat dry with paper towels. Cut beef into chunks and season with salt and pepper.

  4. Heat oil in a Dutch over over medium-high heat. Add beef and cook until browned on all sides for about 10 minutes. Remove from heat.

  5. While the beef is searing, continue the sauce. Line a cast iron grill pan or griddle with aluminum foil and place over high heat. Arrange tomatoes in a single layer on top. Grill until tomato skin is burned on all sides and begins to peel n(3-5 minutes).

  6. Place cooled chile peppers into a blender. Add vinegar, garlic, 2 tsp black pepper, cloves, cinnamon, cumin, thyme, marjoram, oregano, and salt to the blender. Pour in reserved 1/4 cup of chile water and blend until smooth.

  7. Strain chile sauce through a mesh strainer and pour over browned meat in the Dutch oven, turning the roast so it is completely covered with sauce. Cover with lid.

  8. Bake the roast in the preheated oven, basting meat every 45 minutes with sauce. Birria should start to fall apart after about 3-4 hours. Bake uncovered until birria is crispy on top for about 20 minutes. Remove from the oven, cover with 2 layers of aluminum foil, and allow to rest for 10 minutes.

  9. Remove meat from oven and move to a cutting board. Strain off any fat from the sauce. Shred meat with 2 forks, then return to the pot and stir with the sauce to combine.

  10. Fill each tortilla with birria and top with Mexican cheese, chopped onions, and cilantro. Warm tortillas on a griddle, flipping until both sides are crispy and cheese is melted.

  11. Serve with extra sauce on the side for dipping.
