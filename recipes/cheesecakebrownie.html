Cheesecake Brownies

Description

Looking to add some pizzazz to your dessert? Well, if you love cheesecake and brownies, then this recipe is for you. It's just something about a sweet, chocolatey dessert that excites me!

Super fudgy and topped with delicious, creamy cheesecake?! This dish is sure to be a delight. You can also use the box brownie mix and get these results if you follow the batter directions on the box.

Ingredients

Brownies

  • 1 12 oz. bag semi-sweet or bittersweet chocolate chips
  • 6 tbsp unsalted butter (cut into small cubes)
  • 3/4 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 3 eggs (preferably room temperature)
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1/3 cup cornstarch
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 cup mini semi-sweet chocolate chips

Cheesecake Layer

  • 8 oz (softened) cream cheese
  • 1/2 cup granulated sugar
  • 1 egg (preferably room temperature)
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract

Steps

  1. Preheat over to 350 degrees (ºF).

  2. Lightly spray an 9-inch square baking pan with cooking spray. Line with parchment paper leaving an overhang for easy removal. Set aside.

  3. For the brownies, in a large bowl, combine chocolate chips and butter. Melt in 30-second increments, on high heat in the microwave. Stir in between increments.

  4. Stir in sugar and vanilla until completely combined.

  5. Add eggs one at a time, stirring in between until combined.

  6. In a small bowl, combine your dry ingredients. Whisk together cocoa powder, cornstarch, and salt.

  7. Add dry mix to wet mix and stir vigorously until the mixture is smooth and begins to pull away from the sides of the bowl. This will take 1-2 minutes.

  8. Stir in mini chocolate chips.

  9. Pour 2/3 of the brownie batter into the prepared pan and smooth the top with a spatula. Set aside mixture.

  10. For the cheesecake layer, use a mechanical or handheld mixer to beat the cream cheese until light and fluffy.

  11. Add in the sugar and beat until combined.

  12. Beat in the egg and vanilla extract until smooth.

  13. Spoon the cheesecake filling over the top of the brownie batter and carefully spread into a thin layer with baking spatula.

  14. Drizzle or spoon the remaining brownie batter over the top.

  15. Bake for 30-35 minutes or until inserted toothpick comes out with a few moist crumbs.

  16. Let cool completely in pan. Use the parchment paper overhang to pull the brownies out and cut into squares.

  17. Store in an airtight container in the fridge.
